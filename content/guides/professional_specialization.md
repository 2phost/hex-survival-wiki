# Guide: [[mechanisms/skills|Professional Specialization]]

In Hex Survival, no one person can do everything. Success requires the town to coordinate and assign players into specialized "professions."

## 1. Professional Blueprints
These are optimized paths for the town's key personnel.

### The Scout (The Pathfinder)
**Focus**: AP Capacity & Scavenger's Eye.
- **Role**: Map the world, identify key biomes (Oasis, Vault, etc.), and disable active world threats.
- **Skill Priority**: 
    1. **[[mechanisms/skills|AP Capacity]]** (Lvl 5): To allow for deep-world expeditions.
    2. **[[mechanisms/skills|Scavenger's Eye]]** (Lvl 5): To ensure high-value loot is found efficiently during travel.
    3. **[[mechanisms/skills|Rest Recovery]]** (Lvl 5): To maximize AP regeneration during downtime.
- **Items**: **[[mechanisms/items|Starter Lamp]]** (Early), **[[mechanisms/items|Functioning Lamp]]** (Late), and at least 2 **[[mechanisms/items|Stim Packs]]**.

### The Engineer (The Builder)
**Focus**: Field Engineering & Generator Operations.
- **Role**: Maintain the town's power grid, build advanced infrastructure, and operate powered industrial facilities.
- **Skill Priority**: 
    1. **[[mechanisms/skills|Field Engineering]]** (Lvl 3): To operate Industrial and Electronic Lab facilities.
    2. **[[mechanisms/skills|Generator Operations]]** (Lvl 3): To use high-tier fuels like **[[mechanisms/items|Plasma Fuel Rods]]**.
    3. **[[mechanisms/skills|AP Capacity]]** (Lvl 3): To provide enough AP for building complex projects.
- **Items**: **[[mechanisms/items|Gasoline Canisters]]**, **[[mechanisms/items|Copper Wiring]]**, and **[[mechanisms/items|Circuit Boards]]**.

### The Quartermaster (The Scavenger)
**Focus**: Pack Mule & Salvage.
- **Role**: Gather resources from the "Loot Highways," deconstruct items for materials, and manage the town's shared stockpiles.
- **Skill Priority**: 
    1. **[[mechanisms/skills|Pack Mule]]** (Lvl 3): To maximize carrying capacity for bulk scavenging.
    2. **[[mechanisms/skills|Salvage]]** (Lvl 5): To deconstruct junk items into high-tier rare materials.
    3. **[[mechanisms/skills|Scavenger's Eye]]** (Lvl 3): To reduce the time spent searching.
- **Items**: **[[mechanisms/items|Rusty Tools]]**, **[[mechanisms/items|Broken Radios]]**, and any equipment with high deconstruction yields.

### The Survivor (The Sustainer)
**Focus**: Rest Recovery & Stim Conditioning.
- **Role**: Act as the town's "Emergency Responder" for high-threat events and quest objectives.
- **Skill Priority**: 
    1. **[[mechanisms/skills|Rest Recovery]]** (Lvl 5): To stay active for long periods during a siege.
    2. **[[mechanisms/skills|Stim Conditioning]]** (Lvl 3): To use **[[mechanisms/items|Stim Injectors]]** and **[[mechanisms/items|Stim Overdrive]]** effectively.
    3. **[[mechanisms/skills|AP Capacity]]** (Lvl 4): To maximize their emergency response window.

## 2. Managing Training Time
Remember that skill training is a real-time process.
- **Coordination**: Use the Town Chat to ensure the town has at least one player training **[[mechanisms/skills|Field Engineering]]** and **[[mechanisms/skills|Generator Operations]]** by Day 2.
- **Priority**: A Lvl 2 skill (24 hours) is a major commitment. Plan your training around your real-world availability. 
- **The "AP Lock"**: Skill training is free (0 AP) and can be done while resting or scavenging. Never stop training.
